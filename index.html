Context: Los Angeles power mix (LADWP, 2024)

How does LA's actual grid compare? LADWP supplies ~20,700 GWh/yr to 4 million people. Its 2024 mix was 59% carbon-free — but the remaining 41% natural gas still generates significant CO₂ and combustion waste. The chart below shows the waste/emissions burden of each source in LA's mix, plotted on the same scale as the reactor HLW figures above.

LA 2024 grid mix
Natural gas produces ~490 kg CO₂/MWh plus solid combustion residues. At 41% of LADWP's ~20,700 GWh, that's roughly 4.2 million tonnes of CO₂ per year — plus ash and scrubber waste. Nuclear (Palo Verde, 14% of mix) produces only ~3.5 m³ of solid HLW per GWe·yr — the most compact waste stream of any firm power source. Solar and wind produce no operational waste but require land, mining, and panel/blade disposal at end-of-life. LA exited coal in late 2025; the IPP plant in Utah now runs on natural gas blended toward green hydrogen.
Nuclear HLW data: approximate engineering estimates for 1 GWe commercial-scale reactors. FBR and MSR values reflect design targets. LA grid data: LADWP 2024 Power Content Label and annual report. CO₂ equivalents use EPA/IPCC lifecycle factors.
